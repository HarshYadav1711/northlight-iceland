Destinations

Five regions that cover most first visits. Each section notes driving time from Reykjavík, best season, and what makes the route worth your days.

Capital region

Reykjavík

Most trips start here. Keflavík International Airport sits roughly 45 minutes south-west of the city, and Reykjavík works well as a base for day tours.

Allow at least one full day for the city itself: Hallgrímskirkja, Harpa concert hall, and the old harbour area. Restaurants fill quickly on weekends — booking ahead helps.

  • Best for: Short breaks, culture, dining, day-trip hub
  • Typical stay: 2–3 nights
  • Nearby: Golden Circle, Blue Lagoon, Reykjanes Peninsula
Classic day route

Golden Circle

The Golden Circle loops through Þingvellir National Park, Geysir geothermal area, and Gullfoss waterfall. The full circuit is roughly 230 km and fits comfortably in a day from Reykjavík.

Start early if visiting in peak summer. Parking at popular stops fills by mid-morning. Winter driving is usually manageable on main roads, but check road.is before you leave.

Waterfalls and coast

South Coast

Route 1 south from Reykjavík passes Seljalandsfoss and Skógafoss within two hours. Continue to Reynisfjara black-sand beach and the village of Vík for a strong two-day itinerary.

Coastal winds can be strong. Stay behind marked paths at beaches — sneaker waves are a real hazard. Ice on paths is common from November through March.

Peninsula loop

Snæfellsnes Peninsula

Often called "Iceland in miniature," Snæfellsnes packs glaciers, fishing villages, and coastal cliffs into a peninsula roughly two hours north-west of Reykjavík.

Kirkjufell mountain near Grundarfjörður is the most photographed peak. A full loop takes a long day; staying overnight in Stykkishólmur or Arnarstapi reduces driving pressure.

Beyond the capital

North Iceland

Akureyri is the main hub — a compact city with flights from Reykjavík if you prefer not to drive the full ring road. Lake Mývatn, Goðafoss, and whale watching from Húsavík are the headline draws.

Allow at least four days if driving from Reykjavík. Shoulder-season visits (May and September) often balance daylight, crowds, and aurora potential.
