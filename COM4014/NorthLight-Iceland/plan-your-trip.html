Plan Your Trip

Practical decisions first: how long you have, what season you are travelling in, and whether you will drive or rely on tours from Reykjavík.

Seasons

Choose the window that fits your goals

Summer (June–August)

Long daylight, highland road access (F-roads), hiking, and puffin season. Peak prices and crowds. Book accommodation early.

Shoulder (May, September)

Fewer visitors, moderate prices, and a mix of hiking and early aurora potential. Weather shifts quickly — pack layers.

Winter (November–March)

Short days, aurora focus, ice caves, and snow landscapes. Some highland routes close. 4×4 recommended for remote areas.

On the road

Driving in Iceland

Route 1 (the Ring Road) is paved and maintained year-round, though winter storms can close sections temporarily. Gravel roads and single-lane bridges are common outside towns.

Check road.is and vedur.is every morning before long drives. Wind gusts can push vehicles on open stretches — reduce speed and grip the wheel firmly.

  • Speed limits: 50 km/h in urban areas, 80 km/h on gravel, 90 km/h on paved rural roads.
  • F-roads (highland tracks) require a 4×4 and are summer-only.
  • Fuel stations thin out in rural areas — fill up when you can.
Budget

What to expect to spend

Iceland is not a budget destination, but costs vary widely by season and how much you self-cater.

Typical daily costs per person (approximate, ISK)
Category Budget Mid-range Comfort
Accommodation Guesthouse dorm: 5,000–8,000 Hotel / guesthouse room: 15,000–25,000 Boutique hotel: 30,000+
Food Supermarket meals: 3,000–5,000 Café lunch + casual dinner: 8,000–12,000 Restaurant dining: 15,000+
Transport Bus pass / shared tours Compact rental car + fuel 4×4 rental + fuel
Activities Free hikes, public pools One paid tour per day Multiple guided experiences

Prices fluctuate with season and exchange rates. Supermarkets such as Bónus and Krónan offer the best value for self-catering.

Sample routes

Itineraries by trip length

5 days — Reykjavík base

Day 1: Arrive, explore city. Day 2: Golden Circle. Day 3: South Coast to Vík. Day 4: Reykjanes / Blue Lagoon. Day 5: Depart. Works without changing hotels.

7 days — South and Snæfellsnes

Days 1–2: Reykjavík. Days 3–4: South Coast overnight in Vík. Day 5: Snæfellsnes loop. Days 6–7: Return via Golden Circle or extra Reykjavík time.

10–14 days — Ring Road

Full circuit with overnights in Vík, Höfn, Egilsstaðir, Mývatn, and Borgarnes. Allow buffer days for weather. Not recommended in deep winter without 4×4 experience.

Winter focus — Aurora week

Reykjavík plus two rural nights for darker skies. Daylight for Golden Circle or geothermal pools; evenings for aurora attempts. See our Northern Lights guide.

Packing

Essentials by season

All year

  • Waterproof jacket and trousers
  • Sturdy walking boots with ankle support
  • Layers (base, mid, outer)
  • Reusable water bottle — tap water is safe
  • EU Type F plug adapter (230V)

Winter additions

  • Insulated gloves, hat, and thermal base layers
  • Ice grips for boots (optional but helpful)
  • Headlamp for aurora viewing
