Practical basics

What to sort out before you land

The unglamorous details that save time once you are on the island.

When to go

Summer brings long daylight and open highland roads. Winter trades hiking hours for aurora nights and ice caves. Shoulder seasons (May and September) balance both.

Getting around

A rental car is the default for most routes. Ring Road 1 is paved and well maintained; F-roads in the highlands need a 4×4 and close outside summer.

What things cost

Iceland is expensive by most standards. Budget for fuel, food, and accommodation separately — a hostel bunk and a guesthouse room are very different line items.

Staying safe

Weather changes fast and mobile signal is patchy outside towns. Check SafeTravel.is daily, tell someone your route, and never underestimate wind near cliffs.
