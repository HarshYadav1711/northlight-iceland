Destinations

Capital

Reykjavík

Most international flights land at Keflavík, roughly 45 minutes from the city centre. Reykjavík works as a base for day trips, a place to recover from jet lag, and the easiest spot to rent a car before heading out.

Hallgrímskirkja, Harpa concert hall, and the old harbour fill a comfortable day on foot. Restaurants book up on weekends — reserve ahead if you have a short list in mind.

  • Drive from KEF: 45 minutes
  • Ideal stay: 2–3 nights
  • Best season: Year-round

Pair with: Geothermal pools, Whale watching

Day route

Golden Circle

Þingvellir, Geysir, and Gullfoss form the classic loop — about 230 km and manageable in a single day from Reykjavík. Þingvellir sits on the rift between tectonic plates; Strokkur geyser erupts every few minutes; Gullfoss drops in two stages into a canyon.

Arrive before 10:00 in summer to avoid the busiest parking. Check road.is before winter drives — ice and wind close sections without much warning.

  • Drive from Reykjavík: 1–1.5 hours to Þingvellir
  • Ideal stay: 1 day (or overnight nearby)
  • Best season: Year-round

Pair with: Northern Lights (dark skies at Þingvellir)

Coast & waterfalls

South Coast

Route 1 south passes Seljalandsfoss and Skógafoss within two hours of the capital. Continue to Reynisfjara black-sand beach and Vík for a strong two-day route with an overnight stop — or push on toward Jökulsárlón glacier lagoon for a third day.

Coastal winds are fierce year-round. Stay behind marked paths at beaches — sneaker waves at Reynisfjara have caused fatalities. Paths can be icy from November through March.

  • Drive from Reykjavík: 2 hours to Seljalandsfoss
  • Ideal stay: 2–3 days
  • Best season: May–September (ice caves in winter)

Pair with: Glacier hiking, Ice caves

North hub

Akureyri

Iceland's second city sits at the head of Eyjafjörður fjord — compact, walkable, and a practical base for Lake Mývatn, Goðafoss, and whale watching from nearby Húsavík.

Domestic flights from Reykjavík take about 45 minutes if you prefer not to drive the full Ring Road. Allow four to five days minimum if driving from the capital, with an overnight stop along the way.

  • From Reykjavík: 5–6 hour drive / 45 min flight
  • Ideal stay: 3–4 nights
  • Best season: June–September

Pair with: Whale watching, Geothermal pools

Remote west

Westfjords

The least visited corner of Iceland rewards slow travel. Dynjandi waterfall, Látrabjarg bird cliffs, and fishing villages like Ísafjörður feel far from the Ring Road crowds — because they are.

Gravel roads, single-lane tunnels, and long distances mean this region suits a dedicated trip rather than a quick detour. Summer is the practical window for most routes; winter access is limited.

  • Drive from Reykjavík: 5–7 hours to Ísafjörður
  • Ideal stay: 4–5 days
  • Best season: June–August

Pair with: Northern Lights (excellent dark skies)
